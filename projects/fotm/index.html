Festival of the Mind

Identity Graphic design Delivery Project management

Academic research can often feel intimidating or disconnected from the everyday lives of the general public. Festival of the Mind was launched as a biennial celebration in Sheffield that brings together University research and local creative talent.

As the lead designer (2012 - 2024) for the festival’s visual identity, my role was to transform complex ideas into an inviting, high-energy physical experience that resonated with families, children, and residents across the city.

What was needed

We needed to bridge the gap between a person seeing a billboard on a busy street and that same person actually attending an event or visiting the website. To do this the project required a cohesive design system that functioned as both a brand and a wayfinding tool:

  • Clear call-to-action: A streamlined path from physical street-level touchpoints to the digital programme.
  • User-centred visuals: An identity that appealed to diverse demographics, specifically families, without losing the prestige of the University.
  • Production Management: End-to-end oversight of large-scale advertising and print to ensure the user experience remained consistent across every touchpoint.

My approach

I applied a user-centred design lens to the festival’s history:

  • By understanding what resonated (and what didn’t), I refined the branding to be more “scannable.” I treated large-scale adverts like digital landing pages, prioritising information hierarchy so passersby could grasp the “Who, What, and Where” in seconds.
  • I designed everything from 48-sheet billboards to handheld programmes, ensuring that the visual cues remained consistent as the user moved from the street into the exhibition spaces.
  • I managed the full print and production cycle ensuring the festival’s physical presence matched the design intent and provided a seamless experience for the visitor.

The outcome

The result was a vibrant, city-wide takeover that successfully humanised the University’s research. By using an iterative, feedback-led approach, we saw a measurable increase in public engagement and exhibition attendance compared to previous years.

Overview

Central Processing Unit (CPU Records) was established in 2012 with a vision to release high-quality electronic music and create a brand that was instantly recognizable. Inspired by the early days of Warp Records, whose iconic purple sleeves became a symbol of trust and anticipation, CPU aimed to evoke the same sense of curiosity and excitement — where the artwork alone would be enough to prompt listeners to make a purchase, even without hearing the music.

To achieve this, I focused on crafting a visual identity that would resonate with listeners and stand out in a crowded market. Drawing inspiration from the lasting impact of Warp’s design, I developed a minimal and distinctive identity centered around binary code, incorporating it into catalogue numbers and track listings. This became a key element in defining the label’s unmistakable look. Much like the early Warp records, this approach ensured the label’s covers would instantly grab attention and forge a strong visual connection with fans.

Beyond standard 12” vinyl records, I extended the identity across unconventional formats — floppy discs, flexidiscs, and cassettes — as well as merchandise like t-shirts, posters, and books, while also directing live visuals and music video animations.

CPU Records continues to thrive today, releasing music from artists around the globe. They recently celebrated their 128th release — halfway to the full binary system!

Established in 2012, Central Processing Unit (CPU Records) is an electronic music record label with an emphasis on systematic visual identity.

The design requirement was immediate recognisability across releases, similar to early Warp Records sleeves, where visual consistency created a strong identity and signalled quality at a glance. This led me to conceive a minimal system built on binary notation, establishing a clear and repeatable visual language.

I integrated binary code into catalogue numbers and track listings, forming a coherent framework across all releases. The result is a uniform and scalable identity that functions independently of individual artists.

I applied the system across multiple formats, including 12-inch vinyl, floppy disks, flexidiscs and cassettes, as well as printed and digital outputs such as posters, apparel, live visuals and video.

CPU Records remains active, with an ongoing international roster of artists.
